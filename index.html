How African- Americans and People of Color are Disproportionately Targeted by the Police .

Cheyann Harris

Nov. 22 2020

The tragic killing of George Floyd, an unarmed black man, by a white officer highlighted the high levels of racism, discrimination, and abuse against African-Americans and people of color in America. Black people and people of color are often killed by law enforcement at higher and more alarming rates than their white counterparts.

Out of over 100 Police Departments in the country, data has shown that Black people and people of color are killed at higher and more alarming rates than their white counterparts, especially in bigger and more popular cities. All data has been compared between the years 2013-2019 <

In Majority of cities in America, black people are killed at a 50% or higher rate which is the opposite of white Americans.
